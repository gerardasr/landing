Abstract

A glacier is shaped through the accumulation of ice, snow, rock, and sediment. As a being of its own, the glacier triggers our imagination. It’s the difference between inhuman forces and our human fragility that creates the desire to observe, engage, relate, and touch. Landing has evolved through encounters of practices and propositions. We convene around twenty-one submissions presented here. Our collaboration is shaped by close reading and reflections between the editorial team, our advisory board, the authors of Edge of the Glacier’s text/image submissions, and our community of peer reviewers.

Author Biographies

Miklós (Miki) Ambrózy

Miklós (Miki) Ambrózy (he/him) is a Vilnius-based researcher, writer, and artist working with film. He is an active member of the Why Cut When You Can Fade artistic research group, founder of the platform Landing, and a member of the artist-run film lab SPONGĖ. His research topics include degrowth, pollution, poetics, and the transfer of artistic methods. He is currently employed as artistic researcher at Vilnius Academy of Arts. Miki has studied Audiovisual Arts and Film Directing in LUCA School of Arts, Brussels, and acting for stage at the Thessaloniki Centre for Experimental Theatre. Miki's films and installations have been screened in Brussels, Paris, Vilnius, Riga, and Athens.

www.mikiambrozy.com

Risk Hazekamp

Risk Hazekamp (they/them) is a Dutch interdependent visual artist, researcher and art educator. Through a combination of personal activism, decolonial praxis and analogue (currently micro-organic) photography, visual intersectional processes are developed to change existing systems. As of 2023, Risk started their Professional Doctorate research entitled: Unlearning Photography: Listening to Cyanobacteria at the Centre of Applied Research for Art, Design and Technology of Avans University of Applied Sciences.
